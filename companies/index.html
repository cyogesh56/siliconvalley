Twitter is a microblogging and social networking service where users post and interact with messages known as “tweets” Twitter was created in March 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams, with Jack Dorsey is sitting as CEO currently. It is based in San Francisco, California and has more than 25 offices around the world.

Twitter business model is similar to the business model of other social networks. Users create a profile and then those users can post short status updates as well as share videos, images, and links. They can also follow other users and view the tweets of the accounts they choose to follow. Twitter has also began shifting its focus to video content and shares revenue with content creators (70% goes to the content creators, the rest goes to Twitter). It also wants to get exclusive digital rights to big events like NFL games, political debates, award ceremonies, etc. to enhance its business model. From a value proposition, Twitter offers its various stakeholders benefits such as user benefits, advertiser benefits, and data partner benefits.

In terms of innovation resources and investments, Twitter uses a multitude of ways to stay innovative. From an internal perspective, Twitter puts together Hackathons twice a year where employees can give 100% of their time during the Hackathons. They can partner with external groups during this time as well. During times when a Hackathon is not going on, innovation still occurs as each team has its own “innovation team/champion” as well as a governing body. This allows every team to put forth new ideas and have a team that focuses on evaluation for continuing execution. In terms of external innovation, Twitter’s big focus is pulling ideas from users themselves, especially those users who are verified as an account of public interest by Twitter. To continue to move new ideas from idea to production, Twitter utilizes an internal version of their app, nicknamed DogFood. Everyone at Twitter then has the opportunity to use the new features and give feedback.

For external innovation, Twitter uses the same methods we see from companies and firms throughout the US with partnerships with colleges and other incubators. Each agreement comes with NDAs and SOWs so Twitter retains ownership of all developed material.

Google is a multinational, publicly-traded organization built around the company’s hugely popular search engine. Google’s other enterprises include internet analytics, cloud computing, advertising technologies, and web app, browser, and operating system development. Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergrey Brin.

Google’s business strategy and model revolves around the idea that by providing free services to their users, people will continue to use the services as well as bring advertisers with them. By having their revenue come from advertisers who are interested in reaching out to the online users who use the services, internet users never have to incur a fee to resulting in more and more users flocking to Google. To help advertisers, Google helps them create text-based ads through Google Adwords, a self-serve auction based advertising program. Most advertisers pay Google on a Cost per Click (CPC) basis. This means advertisers pay Google whenever users click on their ads. To help advertisers extend their ad campaigns to more of the Google users, Google created its AdSense program where companies are allowed to generate ad revenue on their own and Google only got one-third of the revenue generated.

All innovation from Google comes from its North Star. The idea of ensuring product excellence when comparing their benchmark products against competitors and the idea that it is an incentive to help others reach their goals. Google continues to innovate from the product perspective to this day behind the idea of a “Garage Mindset”. All employees need to understand the why vs. the how for each improvement of current products and new, out of the box ideas. This helps foster a workplace environment where innovation is at the lead. Google has three key points when it comes to bringing in innovative ideas. First, is to focus on the user. Google collects a lot of user feedback and incorporates ideas into products as they know having user support for updates and new products is one of the key driving forces to successful adoption. Second, Google encourages all employees to think 10X. By this they mean, whatever idea is thought of to expand on it a multiple of ten times. This helps the company to push the limit of what can be done and allows for very adventurous ideas to rise up, gain traction, and become new products or features. The third point may be Google’s biggest advantage over other companies. They give their employees the freedom to innovate. This is the foundation of their culture. This can be seen from varying angles. Google was the first company to implement the 20% policy, where employees can give 20% of their time to work on any work they feel passionate about. Alongside the 20% policy, Google has an innovation space called Area 120, which is a virtual as well as a physical presence. Area 120 is an internal incubator of sorts where new innovative ideas can get dedicated resources to assist in building out the idea with structured milestones built in. This allows the ideas to graduate through different tiers with increasing levels of resource allocation. The idea of failing fast and iterate off of the failure also helps them push new products through the innovation pipeline quickly. Google wants to present failures, learn from it, celebrate the failure, and continue to innovate. This can be seen in a new world example of the transition from Google Glass to Google Cardboard. The success of Google Cardboard would not have happened with the failure of Google Glass.

SAP.iO is a software venture capitalist platform for startup companies specifically using SAP products in an innovative way to drive solutions. Founded in March 2017, located in San Francisco, CA. The CEO is Bill McDermott.

The SAP.iO Fund makes strategic investments in external startups who drive customer value from digitization. They are investing in early stage startups who can leverage SAP’s data, APIs and technologies. It also enables SAP customers to realize the new, highly incremental value to investments in SAP solutions.

The SAP.iO Venture Studio invests in small, entrepreneurial teams to build and launch products that attack some of the biggest problems in enterprise and challenge the status quo.
They identify future opportunities and markets today and fund ideas for employee-led innovation. SAP.iO focuses on how to better understand what the customer wants, how to find innovation with growth for tomorrow to remain profitable and how to engage better than a paycheck. After a competitive selection process, founders are empowered to turn innovative ideas into profitable businesses with the focus on solving real problems to help customers stay relevant.

Proving that small input can have a big output. If SAP.iO was not founded SAP as a whole would face struggles in the near future, their ERP tool is valuable but has limitations. SAP.iO brings the innovative piece to the organization and opens up a new market for SAP.

A few of the startups that SAP.iO successfully funded are Plum – integrates SAP SuccessFactors to provide companies with the data they need to hire grow and retain top talent. Comines AI with advances Industrial/Organizational Psychology, BidID – leverages machine learning and identity intelligence to help enterprises operationalize data privacy. Organizations use this information to mitigate risk, monitor usage activity and ensure the private data remains protected, Medal – is health data refined. Medal extracts trapped health data out of disconnect EHR and EMR systems, transform health data into meaningful usable data and aggregates indexed data to provide clear patient narrative.

Headquartered in San Francisco, CA and was founded in 1999 by executives from Oracle introducing Software as a Service (SaaS). Salesforce went public in 2004. The current CEOs are Marc Benioff (1999) and Keith Block (2018).

In 2014, Salesforce announced its Customer Success Platform to tie together Salesforce's services, including sales, service, marketing, analytics, community, and mobile apps. In 2017, they launched a Facebook Analytics tool for B2B. In 2018, Salesforce partnered with Apple to improve apps for businesses. Salesforce was first SaaS now Cloud Computing.

Salesforce has a volunteer time off (VTO) which allows their employees have time to go and volunteer with any cause they desire, they also have access to 10 free Salesforce licenses to be used as they prefer. To remain innovative employees have access to Salesforce’s Trailhead community for learning, reengineering opportunities and gain credentials.
Based on the communication with Salesforce they are lacking an innovative process, I don’t feel the employee based is tapped for innovative ideas to benefit the organization. Salesforce has 3 major releases per year, the employees use the functionality internally prior to the releases and provide feedback to refine the product.

Salesforce has clear strategy and top focus to include trust, customer value, quality success. Their culture includes remembering who you are and what you stand for. They use a V2MOM performance process that aligns with the CEO’s strategy and business plan, all 35,000 employees must complete a V2MOM and align with the CEO and reviewed with their management for accuracy.

Linkedin is professional social networking service that operates via a mobile app or website. Their CEO is Jeff Weiner and was founded in 2003, headquarters is located in Mountain View, CA., current employee count is 14,000 and as of June 2019, LinkedIn had 630 million registered members in 200 countries.

On June 13, 2016, Microsoft announced that it would acquire LinkedIn for $196 a share, a total value of $26.2 billion. Microsoft would allow LinkedIn to "retain its distinct brand, culture and independence", with Weiner to remain as CEO, who would then report to Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella. Analysts believed Microsoft saw the opportunity to integrate LinkedIn with its Office product suite to help better integrate the professional network system with its products. The deal was completed on December 8, 2016 [1].

When we talk about LinkedIn we will be talking about their Market space, there is an Enterprise side that consist of HR and more, but in this analysis it’ll be solely on the market side. Researchers at Linkedin have visibility across the company and teams are constructed of product owners, engineers, data scientists, marking professional and currently there’s 200 designers with approximately 40 researchers. The number of years a employee stays at LinkedIn is an average of 4 years but they allow for employees to rotate to other departments, to not get burned out, and allow them to allocate 20% of their time to help with innovational ideas/projects. Hackathon is one outlet that they host annually where employees can propose an idea and other resource can work on this idea. There are N days that consists of one Friday in the month where its time back to the employees to work on another project, propose an idea or simply take the day off. There is even a Patent program that engineers are given a patent in their name for the code or development they created.

LinkedIn’s product innovation is driven by the business objectives, the business will have goals set and based on those goals there’s features, products and such that roll up and there’s different input and outputs that are approved and funded for implementation. Voice of the member is utilized, which is an aggregated marketed tool, collect information and looking through that data to analysis some weak areas, or from their research they’ll flag items that need more investigation. Product has to be involved in the research so that they can hear the feedback from the research that is being conducted.

QR Code was not on a product roadmap and that feature was introduced by watching people exchange their phones and manually enter their names so they can connect on LinkedIn. The product manager and researcher thought this would be great feature to introduce in the Search field and being that the product manager is from China, utilizes WeChat, the decision was to use QR Codes. Another feature that was recently deployed is Event, that took a year and half to build out and partnered with Microsoft to be in the conferences to demo their MVP and pilot launched to gather research. They have different approaches when before implementation, such as a company release, small subset of accounts and ten or twenty percent.

This idea originated when the CEO, Nick Ionita, and his cofounder came up with a thesis and felt that résumés and job descriptions are broken, people don’t write them well and they’re artifacts of the industrial era. Everything today is knowledge work, résumés are pointed to getting a job and doesn't convey the potential. Job descriptions are poorly written when it should be what the business needs, not who knows how to use Microsoft Office.

The thesis started off with the mindset that the data needed to be better, and if you can look at people in the workspace the same way like this component parts then you can build a better system for matching people opportunity instead of using titles. Their model is to sell the head of HR, to CEOs, CFO and head of business Units and their business problem their effectively solving is Retention, if you can help people to find paths to a new opportunity and fell like their progressing they’ll stay longer in the company.

The product roadmap, for building an enterprise system, they release once a week but doesn't touch critical process. Everything is a unit of opportunity and everything that you build needs to be useable by the greatest number of customers and the only way to make this cheaper everything has to be reusable.
This product, not being a mature one yet, needs to see real data to be able to build a product around and likes to set things out like a theme and a strategy. Its very important that everyone in the company understand that strategy and why they’re picking certain changes, it gets really hard to keep people on board when they can’t understand the strategy and how that’s tracking to the roadmap, so he communicates these on a monthly sprint call.

They want to be an operating system for resourcing and think this starts with all the opportunities and employees have to live in the same system, its a supply and demand comparison exercise that’ll be done. Uber Technologies, Inc. is an American multinational transportation network company offering services that include peer-to-peer ridesharing, ride service hailing, food delivery, and a bicycle-sharing system. The company is based in San Francisco and has operations in over 785 metropolitan areas worldwide.

Ubers approach to disruptive innovation starts with Understanding the problem, distill the problem to simple form resulting in solutions that are simple.

At UBER pursuing innovative ideas is tied to ROI, employees or leadership comes up with a brilliant idea, which when seen from a product perspective or an engineering perspective is great, although the question that rises is what is the ROI in allocating resources to get the idea done, the key metrics.

At UBER, when a new feature comes out, a product manager writes a document called the PRD or Product Revenue Document. Since UBER is a large organisation, there are a lot of features that are coming out in their products, hence the PRD document is shared with everyone who is involved with the feature and responsible for , for example from taxes to the more ram to the finance teams, so that everyone is aware of the impact. Innovative ideas at UBER is dependent on what is the problem you are trying to solve and what are the KPIs you really are trying to drive from.

Ubers approach to innovation is they pursue tangential kind of ideas in an incubator that is set up within Uber. To take the idea ahead funding is available for very fascinating ideas like the flying taxis, which sounds like science fiction. Some of the products Uber is strategically investing on is UBER freight to transport goods from one place to another, JUMP bikes. Although the idea is to have these products separated from the mainstream Uber app.

At Uber, for innovation 20% of employee time is spent on working on innovative ideas , which is called the engineering excellence.
